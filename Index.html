What Is BMI?

Body mass index (BMI) is a value derived from the mass (weight) and height of a person.
The BMI is defined as the body mass divided by the square of the body height, and is expressed in units of kg/m2,
resulting from mass in kilograms and height in metres.

A BMI of less than 18.5 means that a person is Underweight.
A BMI of between 18.5 nad 24.9 is Ideal. A BMI of between 25 and 29.9 is Overweight.
A BMI of over 30 indicates Obesity.
